Heritage Sites

India, home to numerous cultural places offering a spectacular window to the true heritage, here are some of the best travel packages
to enlighten youreslf on the rich history.







Sanchi, Madhya Pradesh

Sanchi is a small town situated near Bhopal. The ancient stupas and monasteries have increased the viewership in Sanchi. For people who have faith in God and love sheer craftsmanship, Sanchi is the best destination. Commissioned by Ashoka the Great, the monuments at Sanchi are the oldest Buddhist structures in India. The 54 feet high stupa gives insight into the life of Lord Buddha.


Ajanta Ellora Caves, Maharashtra

Beautifully carved into hillside rock in the middle of nowhere are the Ajanta and Ellora caves. This famous rock-cut cave is situated in Aurangabad district of Maharashtra. The cave shrines dated back to the 2nd century BC and were created with the hands of the artists of that time. There are 29 caves at Ajanta, carved to describe the story of Buddhism.


Sun Temple, Konarak, Orissa

The Konark or Konarak Sun temple, also known as Black Pagoda, is dedicated to the Hindu sun god Surya and, conceived as a giant stone chariot with 12 wheels, it is the most famous of the few sun temples built in India. Mahabalipuram Shore, Tamil Nadu

Mahabalipuram, famously known as Mamallapuram, is situated on the Coromandel Coast along the Bay of Bengal in Tamil Nadu. Once the home of demon king Mahabali, it was later named as Mamallapuram. It dates back to the 7th century, the rock-cut shore architecture and stone carvings display the Pallava art.


Taj Mahal, U.P

Taj Mahal, listed as one of the seven wonders of the world it is situated on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, it is considered as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world, which was built in the memory of his third wife, Mumtaz.

The city of ruins, as we call it, is a city situated in the hills and valleys of the beautiful state of Karnataka. Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagar empire, located by the Tungabhadra river.
